Introduction

The Polynesian Maori reached New Zealand sometime between A.D. 1250 and 1300.

In 1840, the chieftans entered into a compact with Great Britain called the Treaty of Waitang. In this treaty they ceded sovereignty to Queen Victoria while retaining territorial rights. That same year, the British began the first organized colonial settlement. Between 1843 and 1872, there was a series of land wars that ended with the defeat of the native people.

In 1907, the British colony of New Zealand became an independent dominion and supported the UK militarily in both world wars. New Zealand's full participation in a number of defense alliances lapsed by the 1980s. In recent years, the government has sought to address longstanding Maori grievances.

Independence

New Zealand gained their independence from the UK on September 26, 1907.

Legal System

New Zealand has a common law system that is based on the English model but also contains a special legislation and land courts for the Maori.

To learn more about their government, you can visit their website at: www.govt.nz.
